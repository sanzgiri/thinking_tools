# Boys from Brazil

## Brief Description

The "Boys from Brazil" is a philosophical thought experiment that challenges the notion of genetic determinism, arguing that environment plays a significant role in shaping an individual's character and behavior. Named after the novel "The Boys from Brazil" by Ira Levin, it suggests that even if you clone Hitler, his environment would prevent him from becoming another Hitler.

## Detailed Description

In the context of free will, the "Boys from Brazil" thought experiment poses a question: If we were to clone Adolf Hitler and raise him exactly as he was raised under Nazi Germany, would he become another Hitler? The answer suggested by this tool is no, because environment plays a crucial role in shaping an individual's character.

This thought experiment challenges genetic determinism, the belief that an individual's genes largely determine their behavior and fate. It emphasizes the power of nurture over nature, arguing that even if we have the same genetic makeup, our experiences, upbringing, and environment can lead us down vastly different paths.

This tool is important because it encourages us to question the oversimplification of complex issues like free will, personality, and behavior. It highlights the need to consider both nature and nurture when understanding human behavior and makes a case for optimism in the face of seemingly inevitable repetitions of history.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice this tool, you can engage in a thought experiment or exercise called "Nurture Sim." Here's how:

1. Choose a historical figure known for their extreme characteristics, such as a dictator or a revolutionary leader.
2. Clone this individual.
3. Imagine raising the clone in a completely different environment from the original. For example, raise your Hitler clone as a baker in a peaceful democratic society.
4. Consider how the new environment might influence the clone's behavior and choices. Would they become a baker like their upbringing suggests, or would they be influenced by other factors to follow a different path?

This exercise encourages you to think critically about the role of environment in shaping individuals and question the idea that our genes determine our destiny.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

To gamify this concept, an app could be designed as follows:

1. **Profile Creation:** Users can choose from a list of historical figures known for their extreme characteristics. Each figure comes with a brief description of their life and actions.
2. **Cloning:** After selecting a figure, users are given the clone. They can name the clone and customize its appearance.
3. **Environment Selection:** Users can choose different environments to raise their clones in. Environments could range from war-torn countries to peaceful democracies, with each environment having unique characteristics that influence the clone's development.
4. **Raising the Clone:** Users make decisions for their clone, such as what they learn, who they interact with, and how they spend their time. These decisions shape the clone's personality and behavior.
5. **Observation and Analysis:** As the clone grows, users can observe its development and analyze how the environment has influenced its behavior. The app could provide insights based on psychological and sociological principles to help users understand the impact of nurture.
6. **Compare Clones:** Users can compare their clones with others who have chosen different environments, fostering discussion and learning.
7. **Leaderboards and Achievements:** To make the exercise more engaging, the app could include leaderboards and achievements based on the success of the clones raised. This gamification encourages users to think critically and experiment with different environments.
