# Competence w/o Comprehension

## Brief Description

Competence without Comprehension (CwC) is a thinking tool that emphasizes the ability to perform a task effectively before fully understanding its underlying concepts or principles. This tool encourages us to recognize that many complex systems, patterns, and phenomena can emerge from simple rules and interactions, even when the participants themselves do not possess an explicit understanding of their role in the larger system.

## Detailed Description

Before Darwin's theory of evolution, it was widely believed that intricate designs found in nature (such as the nest of a termite or the eye of an organism) could only be created by an intelligent designer. The concept of CwC challenges this view by demonstrating that complex structures and behaviors can emerge from simple rules and processes without any conscious intention or understanding on the part of the participants.

This philosophical idea highlights the power of emergence, where complex patterns arise from the interaction of numerous simple components. By focusing on competence over comprehension, we can better appreciate the intricate workings of the world around us and develop a more nuanced understanding of complexity and intelligence.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice this thinking tool, users can engage in various exercises that simulate CwC scenarios. One example is The Termite Simulation, as described in Dennett's book "Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking." In this exercise, users define simple rules for virtual entities (representing termites) to follow, such as "Drop pheromone when a certain condition is met." By observing the emergent structures created by these entities, users can appreciate how complex patterns can arise from simple interactions without conscious intent or understanding.

To make this exercise more engaging, users could be presented with different scenarios and challenges, allowing them to experiment with various rule sets and observe the resulting structures' evolution over time. This hands-on approach encourages active learning and promotes a deeper understanding of CwC.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

Building an app based on the concept of CwC can be an engaging and educational experience for users. The app could feature various simulations, allowing users to experiment with different rule sets and observe the emergent structures that result.

The app's main screen could display a selection of pre-defined scenarios (such as termites building nests or flocks of birds forming patterns), along with an option for users to create their custom simulations. Users could define rules for the entities within these simulations using intuitive interfaces, such as sliders or toggles, making it easy to adjust parameters and experiment with various rule sets.

To gamify the experience, the app could include achievements, leaderboards, and challenges related to each scenario. For example, users could compete to create the most intricate structure within a set time frame or with a specific set of rules. This competitive aspect would encourage active engagement and foster a deeper understanding of CwC.

Additionally, the app could provide educational resources on the philosophical concepts underlying CwC, such as emergence, self-organization, and evolution, helping users to gain a more comprehensive understanding of this powerful thinking tool.
