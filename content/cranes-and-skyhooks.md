# Cranes and Skyhooks

## Brief Description

Cranes and Skyhooks is a crucial metaphor in philosophy, particularly relevant to understanding evolution and the nature of explanation. The metaphor differentiates between natural processes (Cranes) that build upon a solid foundation and miraculous explanations (Skyhooks) that seemingly lift design from nowhere.

## Detailed Description

Daniel Dennett introduced the Cranes and Skyhooks metaphor in his book "Darwin's Dangerous Idea" to argue against arguments for intelligent design and supernatural intervention. The metaphor is derived from the image of a construction site, where cranes are mechanical structures that lift materials built on a solid foundation, while skyhooks appear as magic hooks that lift design out of nowhere.

The philosophical problem this tool solves revolves around understanding how complex structures can evolve naturally without the need for supernatural intervention. Cranes represent natural selection and the various mechanisms (such as sex and culture) it uses to adapt species over time, building upon an existing foundation. Skyhooks, on the other hand, are used to explain phenomena that seem too complex or intricate to have evolved naturally, but do so by invoking supernatural or otherwise unexplained processes.

The importance of Cranes and Skyhooks lies in its ability to provide a framework for evaluating explanations and theories. By understanding that evolutionary processes are like cranes, we can appreciate the slow, resource-heavy, yet inherently natural process by which complex structures emerge. This helps us avoid the temptation of invoking supernatural or miraculous explanations (skyhooks) when natural ones are available.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice using Cranes and Skyhooks, it is helpful to engage in exercises that require you to explain complex phenomena without relying on supernatural explanations. One such exercise, based on Dennett's "The Tower Builder," is as follows:

1. Imagine you are a builder tasked with creating a tall tower from simple building blocks.
2. You have two types of tools at your disposal: Cranes (slow, resource-heavy but natural) and Skyhooks (fast, resource-light but supernatural).
3. Your goal is to build the tallest tower possible using only Cranes. However, if you use a Skyhook, the level will be disqualified.
4. This exercise encourages you to think about how to build from the bottom up using natural processes rather than relying on miraculous explanations that seem too good to be true.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

The Cranes and Skyhooks concept could be gamified in a mobile or web application with the following features:

1. **Puzzle levels**: Users are presented with various challenges, such as building towers, creating ecosystems, or designing complex machinery. These puzzles require users to use natural processes (Cranes) to solve them, encouraging critical thinking and avoiding the use of supernatural explanations (Skyhooks).
2. **Rewards system**: Users earn points or virtual rewards for solving levels using Cranes, providing a sense of accomplishment and motivation to continue practicing the skill.
3. **Educational resources**: The app could include educational materials that explain the concept of Cranes and Skyhooks, as well as real-world examples of natural vs. supernatural explanations.
4. **Discussion forums**: Users can discuss their solutions, share strategies, and debate the merits of different explanations in a friendly, collaborative environment.
5. **Progress tracking**: The app should track users' progress, allowing them to see how they improve over time and identify areas where they may need to focus more attention.
6. **Customizable difficulty levels**: Users can adjust the complexity of the puzzles to suit their level of understanding and challenge themselves as they become more familiar with Cranes and Skyhooks.
