# Faustian Bargain

## Brief Description

Faustian Bargain is a philosophical tool that encourages clarity over obscurity. It emphasizes the importance of clear communication and avoidance of needless complexity, which can often be traded for professional success or acceptance in academic circles. The term originates from a legendary deal with the devil, symbolizing the exchange of something valuable (e.g., one's soul) for something coveted (e.g., knowledge, power).

## Detailed Description

In academic and intellectual discourse, the Faustian Bargain refers to the tendency to use complex language, jargon, or unnecessary abstractions to appear more knowledgeable or impressive. However, this approach can lead to misunderstandings, confusion, and a lack of accessibility for those outside the specific field or circle.

Daniel Dennett, in his book "Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking," warns against falling into the trap of such an agreement. He argues that clarity and simplicity are essential for effective communication and progress in any discipline, as they facilitate understanding and collaboration among scholars and laypeople alike.

A clear example of this can be found in the realm of scientific research. When findings are communicated using technical jargon and complex language, the general public may struggle to comprehend their implications or relevance, hindering the dissemination of valuable knowledge. On the other hand, translating complex ideas into simple, accessible terms helps bridge the gap between specialists and non-specialists, ensuring that everyone benefits from the fruits of intellectual labor.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice the Faustian Bargain, one can adopt a De-Jargonizer mindset when encountering complex texts or explanations. Here's how you can do it:

1. Identify an academic paper or a piece of intellectual discourse that contains technical jargon and complex language.
2. Translate the text into simple, everyday language, ensuring that the original meaning remains intact. You may need to consult with experts in the field to ensure accuracy.
3. Compare the original text with your simplified version to identify areas of improvement and clarify any ambiguities.
4. Share your results with others and discuss potential ways to further simplify the language without losing its essence.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

A Faustian Bargain app could gamify the de-jargonization process, making it both fun and educational for users. Here's how such an app could be designed:

1. **Academic Text Library**: The app would include a library of academic texts, scientific papers, or intellectual discourses that contain complex language and technical jargon. Users could submit their own texts as well.

2. **De-Jargonizer Mode**: This feature would challenge users to translate the text into simple, everyday language within a time limit, encouraging them to think creatively and critically. Users can earn points based on the difficulty level of the text and the quality of their translations.

3. **Peer Review**: Other users could review the simplified texts and provide feedback, helping improve the clarity and accuracy of the translations. This would foster a collaborative learning environment.

4. **Leaderboard**: A leaderboard showcasing the top de-jargonizers can motivate users to continuously refine their skills and compete with others.

5. **Course Curriculum**: The app could also include lessons on the importance of clarity, effective communication strategies, and techniques for simplifying complex ideas.

By making the process enjoyable, an Faustian Bargain app can help users hone their ability to communicate clearly while promoting a more accessible, understandable academic world.
