# Herb, Alice, Hal

## Brief Description

The thinking tool "Herb, Alice, Hal" is a metaphorical example used to illustrate genetic trade-offs and the complexity of genetics in evolutionary biology, as described by philosopher Daniel Dennett. In this thought experiment, two individuals named Herb and Alice attempt to create a baby (Hal) with desired traits by selectively choosing genes, but soon realize that these genes have multiple effects (pleiotropy), which can lead to unintended consequences.

## Detailed Description

The philosophical concept underlying "Herb, Alice, Hal" is the idea of emergent properties in complex systems. In this case, genes possess multiple functions that may not be fully understood or predictable when trying to manipulate them for specific traits. This tool emphasizes the challenges of top-down design and the importance of understanding the underlying mechanisms of a system before attempting to engineer it.

Herb and Alice represent humans who believe they can control the genetic makeup of their offspring, selecting specific genes for desirable traits like intelligence (IQ). However, these genes also have side effects that may be less desirable, such as frailty or other health issues. This thought experiment highlights the complexity of genetics and the limitations of human knowledge in predicting the outcomes of genetic manipulation.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice the "Herb, Alice, Hal" thinking tool, consider the following exercise:

1. Identify a trait you would like to enhance (e.g., strength, intelligence, agility).
2. List several genes that are believed to contribute to this trait.
3. Research the known effects and side effects of these genes. Remember, genes often have multiple functions.
4. Imagine creating a 'character' with maxed-out traits in your selected area. Consider the potential trade-offs or unintended consequences this would entail based on the side effects of the genes you have chosen.
5. Reflect on what this exercise teaches you about the complexity of genetics and the limitations of top-down design.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

Building an app around "Herb, Alice, Hal" could provide a fun and engaging way to learn about genetic trade-offs. Here's a concept for such an app:

1. **Character Creator**: Users can create their own characters with customizable traits like strength, intelligence, agility, etc., similar to popular RPG games.
2. **Hidden Side Effects**: Each trait upgrade comes with hidden side effects that negatively impact other traits or add unique quirks to the character. For example, increasing strength might lead to a decrease in agility or an increased chance of injury.
3. **Knowledge Base**: Include a knowledge base where users can learn about real-world genes and their functions, as well as their known side effects and interactions with other genes.
4. **Challenges and Quests**: Provide challenges and quests that require users to balance desirable traits against undesirable side effects, encouraging them to think critically about the complexity of genetics.
5. **Leaderboards and Achievements**: Incorporate competitive elements like leaderboards and achievements to motivate users to continue learning and thinking deeply about genetic trade-offs.

By gamifying the concept of "Herb, Alice, Hal," this app would make it accessible and enjoyable for people to explore the intricacies of genetics and better understand the limitations of top-down design in engineering complex systems.
