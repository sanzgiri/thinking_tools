# Locusts and Primes

## Brief Description

The thinking tool "Locusts and Primes" is a metaphor that illustrates how evolution can act like mathematics, even without the organisms involved understanding the mathematical concepts. The example of cicadas emerging in prime-numbered years (13, 17) demonstrates this principle, as these insects have adapted to minimize synchronization with their predators' cycles through an inherent property of prime numbers.

## Detailed Description

The philosophical concept behind "Locusts and Primes" lies in the emergence of complex patterns and adaptations without the necessity for conscious comprehension. In this example, cicadas have evolved to emerge at prime-numbered intervals (13, 17) as a response to predator cycles, even though they are not aware of the mathematical properties involved. This phenomenon highlights the power of natural selection and evolution's ability to create sophisticated adaptations that resemble abstract mathematical concepts.

This concept is crucial for understanding the complexity of life and its emergence from simple beginnings. It challenges the anthropocentric view that only conscious beings can produce complex patterns or behaviors, emphasizing instead that nature has intrinsic properties that lead to such emergent phenomena.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice this thinking tool, users can engage in an exercise similar to the "Predator Cycle" simulation suggested in the base description. In this activity:

1. Set life cycles for a prey species and a predator species.
2. Experiment with different cycle lengths to observe how overlaps between the species affect their survival rates.
3. Observe that if the user selects non-primes (e.g., 12), the "Predator" will eat the "Prey" every cycle, leading to a significant decrease in the Prey population. In contrast, if the user selects primes (e.g., 13), the Predator and Prey cycles rarely overlap, resulting in a more stable Prey population.
4. Reflect on the implications of these observations for real-world ecosystems and the role of prime numbers in determining species survival.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

An app based on the "Locusts and Primes" thinking tool could offer a fun, interactive way to explore this concept. The app would include features such as:

1. A customizable ecosystem with various Prey and Predator species, each having different life cycles (Prime or Non-Prime).
2. Visual representations of the populations and their interactions over time.
3. An option for users to manipulate environmental factors like food availability, climate, or predator behaviors to observe how these changes affect the Prey and Predator populations.
4. Gamification elements such as earning points for discovering prime cycles that maximize prey survival, unlocking new species, or competing with friends on leaderboards.
5. Resources providing additional information about prime numbers, evolution, and their interplay in ecosystems to further deepen users' understanding of the concept.
