# Mitochondrial Eve

## Brief Description
Mitochondrial Eve is a thought tool that helps us understand evolutionary lineage and survival through a unique perspective. The term doesn't refer to the first woman, but rather the last surviving member of an unbroken matrilineal line reaching back to the origins of humanity. This concept sheds light on how certain genes have shaped and influenced our collective history.

## Detailed Description
Dennett uses Mitochondrial Eve as an intuition pump to explain evolutionary biology in a simplified yet profound manner. The tool illustrates that all humans alive today can trace their matrilineal lineage back to one woman, known as Mitochondrial Eve, who lived approximately 200,000 years ago. This concept is crucial because it highlights the interconnectedness of human evolution and the role of survival through gene pairing in shaping our collective lineage.

One fascinating aspect of Mitochondrial Eve is the notion of "Widowmaker" genes—genes that are fatal if not paired correctly, driving certain lineages to die out over time while shaping the ones that survive. This concept emphasizes the importance of genetic compatibility in our evolutionary history and serves as a reminder of the delicate balance necessary for human survival.

## Exercise / How to Apply
To practice this thought tool, one can engage in a mental exercise known as "Lineage Battle Royale." Imagine a graph representing different lineages, each represented by a line. As generations pass, lines die out due to various reasons, such as extinction events or the death of carriers of certain "Widowmaker" genes. The last remaining line is crowned "Eve," symbolizing the last survivor of an unbroken matrilineal line reaching back to our collective origins.

To make this exercise more engaging, one can create a family tree and trace their own matrilineal lineage, contemplating how events in history have affected its continuity. One can also imagine various scenarios where certain "Widowmaker" genes are introduced and observe the impact on different lineages.

## Suggestion for Creating an App
To gamify the Mitochondrial Eve concept, one could create an interactive app that allows users to simulate and visualize various evolutionary scenarios. The app would feature a graph-based interface where users can create their own lineages by introducing different genes with varying degrees of compatibility. As generations pass, lines die out, and the last surviving line is crowned "Eve."

Additional features could include historical events that affect the survival of certain lineages, educational resources about Mitochondrial Eve and its importance in understanding human evolution, and opportunities for users to share their simulated scenarios with others. By making the concept engaging and interactive, this app would not only help users better understand evolutionary biology but also foster a deeper appreciation for our collective history and the delicate balance that has shaped it.
