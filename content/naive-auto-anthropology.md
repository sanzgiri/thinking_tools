# Naive Auto-anthropology

## Brief Description
Naive Auto-anthropology is a thinking tool that encourages individuals to acknowledge and question their own intuitions, recognizing them as personal data rather than universal truths. This tool aims to counteract the common tendency among philosophers (and people in general) to generalize from their individual experiences.

## Detailed Description
Philosophy often starts with introspection, examining one's own thoughts and feelings to arrive at fundamental principles. However, Daniel Dennett argues that this approach can lead to a form of "naive anthropology," where our personal intuitions are mistakenly assumed to be shared by all humans. Naive Auto-anthropology challenges this assumption, advocating for a more objective and empirical approach to understanding human nature.

The importance of Naive Auto-anthropology lies in its potential to foster humility, openness, and critical thinking. By recognizing our intuitions as data points rather than absolutes, we can avoid overgeneralization, prejudice, and dogmatism. This tool encourages us to seek out diverse perspectives, empirical evidence, and rational arguments to arrive at more robust and universally applicable insights.

## Exercise / How to Apply
To practice Naive Auto-anthropology, follow these steps:
1. Identify a personal intuition or belief about human nature, ethics, or other philosophical topics.
2. Reflect on the origin of this intuition. Is it based on personal experience, cultural conditioning, or rational inference?
3. Consider alternative perspectives that contradict your intuition. Where do these perspectives come from? What evidence supports them?
4. Seek out empirical data and rational arguments to evaluate the strength of your intuition and those alternatives.
5. Revise your belief based on this evaluation, acknowledging its provisional nature and openness to revision in light of new evidence.

## Suggestion for Creating an App
An app built around Naive Auto-anthropology could take the form of a survey platform where users answer questions about their intuitions and beliefs. The app would then compare their responses with those of other users worldwide, allowing them to see the global distribution of answers and realize that they may be outliers.

Key features of such an app might include:
1. A library of philosophical questions covering a wide range of topics (ethics, epistemology, metaphysics, etc.).
2. Anonymized data sharing with other users to promote honest and open responses.
3. Gamification elements, such as badges or leaderboards, to incentivize participation and encourage users to engage in critical thinking.
4. Resources for further reading, including articles, essays, and videos, to help users explore their intuitions in greater depth and refine their philosophical understanding.

By using Naive Auto-anthropology as a foundation, this app could empower users to question their assumptions, broaden their perspectives, and engage in constructive philosophical discourse with others from diverse backgrounds.
