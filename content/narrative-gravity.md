# Narrative Gravity

## Brief Description
Narrative Gravity is a philosophical tool that treats the Self as an abstraction, similar to the Center of Gravity in physics. It posits that our identity or sense of self is not a tangible entity, but rather the central point around which we construct and narrate our personal stories.

## Detailed Description
Dennett's Narrative Gravity addresses the complex nature of consciousness and selfhood by proposing a metaphorical framework. By viewing the Self as an abstract concept rather than a physical object, we can better understand how our identities evolve over time, incorporate multiple perspectives, and accommodate contradictions.

The problem this tool solves lies in our inherent need to define and understand ourselves, as well as the limitations of traditional definitions that view the Self as an isolated, unchanging entity. Narrative Gravity helps us to recognize that our identities are dynamic and fluid, shaped by the narratives we construct about ourselves over time.

The importance of this tool lies in its potential to promote self-awareness, empathy, and adaptability. By recognizing the abstraction of the Self, we can more easily accept and integrate new experiences, understand others' perspectives, and develop resilience in the face of change.

## Exercise / How to Apply
To practice Narrative Gravity, follow these steps:

1. Reflect on a significant life event or transition that altered your sense of self.
2. Write a narrative about this experience, focusing on how you described yourself before and after the event.
3. Identify the central themes or characteristics that remained consistent throughout your narrative. These are likely aspects of your "Self" as an abstraction.
4. Consider any contradictions or changes in your narrative that challenge traditional definitions of selfhood. How did these experiences reshape your understanding of who you are?
5. Repeat this exercise with various life events, observing patterns and changes in the central themes of your narratives over time.

## Suggestion for Creating an App
An app based on Narrative Gravity could be designed to help users practice self-reflection and cultivate a deeper understanding of their identities. Features might include:

1. Prompts for writing personal narratives about significant life events or transitions.
2. Guided exercises to help users identify central themes in their narratives and explore how these themes have evolved over time.
3. Gamification elements, such as points or badges, to encourage regular use and foster a sense of accomplishment.
4. Social features that allow users to share their narratives with others, fostering empathy and understanding through the exchange of personal stories.
5. Personalized insights and reflections based on users' narrative data, offering guidance and encouragement for continued self-exploration.

By providing a user-friendly platform for practicing Narrative Gravity, such an app could help individuals develop greater self-awareness, empathy, and adaptability, ultimately contributing to personal growth and well-being.
