# Occam's Broom

## Brief Description

Occam's Broom is a thinking tool coined by philosopher Daniel Dennett, which highlights the intellectual dishonesty of ignoring or suppressing inconvenient facts to support a preferred argument or belief. It is the reverse of the well-known principle, Occam's Razor, which favors simple explanations over complex ones.

## Detailed Description

Occam's Broom serves as a countermeasure against overly simplified or flawed arguments that appear compelling due to their neglect of contradictory evidence. The tool is particularly relevant in situations where individuals intentionally omit crucial facts to support their theories, such as conspiracy theorists and creationists constructing elaborate explanations for phenomena that overlook existing evidence counteracting their arguments.

By using Occam's Broom, individuals can develop a critical thinking habit that fosters skepticism towards seemingly perfect theories and encourages an open-minded examination of all available information. This tool is essential in our increasingly complex and information-rich world, as it helps protect us from falling prey to misinformation, confirmation bias, and other cognitive pitfalls.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice Occam's Broom effectively:

1. Identify a persuasive argument or theory that seems logical yet lacks credibility due to its simplification or omission of key facts. Examples can be found in various domains, such as politics, religion, science, and history.

2. Gather as much relevant information as possible about the topic at hand. This may involve researching multiple sources, considering various perspectives, and evaluating the credibility of each source.

3. Examine the argument for any signs of selective omission or simplification of facts that might weaken its validity. Consider asking questions such as: "What evidence is being ignored?", "Are there alternative explanations that have been overlooked?", and "Why might certain facts be considered inconvenient?"

4. Compare the argument with the available evidence, looking for inconsistencies or gaps that support the application of Occam's Broom. If the argument ignores essential facts that dismantle its logic, label it as "Broomed."

5. Reflect on the importance of thoroughly examining all available information when evaluating arguments and theories to avoid being misled by intellectual dishonesty or deception.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

An Occam's Broom app could serve as a valuable tool for encouraging critical thinking, helping users identify logical fallacies, and fostering an informed citizenry. Features might include:

- A curated news feed of current events, political debates, scientific discoveries, and historical controversies.
- A fact-checking database that verifies the credibility of sources and provides relevant information on various topics.
- An interactive exercise section where users can test their ability to recognize "Broomed" arguments by reading persuasive texts and identifying omitted facts.
- Gamification elements, such as badges or points, to motivate users and make learning fun.
- A community forum for users to discuss topics, share insights, and collaboratively analyze arguments.

By combining these features, an Occam's Broom app could help users develop essential critical thinking skills, promote informed decision-making, and combat the spread of misinformation in our digital age.
