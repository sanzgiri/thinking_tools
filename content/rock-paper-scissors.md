# Rock Paper Scissors

## Brief Description

Rock Paper Scissors (RPS) is a philosophical tool that illustrates the idea of unpredictability as a form of freedom, particularly within the context of free will. In this game, each move has its counter, ensuring that an opponent cannot predict your next action, thus creating "elbow room" for personal agency.

## Detailed Description

Dennett's RPS tool draws inspiration from evolutionary biology, suggesting that creatures develop pseudo-randomness to evade predators. This unpredictability is seen as the freedom of free will, as it allows individuals to make choices that cannot be anticipated by others, ensuring a degree of independence. In the context of RPS, winning is achieved by being unpredictable and breaking the opponent's pattern of thought.

The philosophical importance of RPS lies in its ability to bridge the gap between science (which often views actions as determined by laws of nature) and free will (the belief that individuals have the power to make choices). By demonstrating how unpredictability can be a source of freedom, RPS challenges deterministic views and encourages us to explore our personal agency.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice this tool, engage in repeated games of RPS with a partner or an AI opponent. Initially, stick to simple patterns (such as always choosing rock) to help your opponent predict your moves. Gradually introduce randomness by occasionally switching between rock, paper, and scissors. The goal is not necessarily to win every game but rather to become less predictable over time.

As you progress, experiment with more complex strategies, such as observing your opponent's patterns and adapting your choices accordingly. The ultimate challenge is to create a pattern that your opponent cannot predict while still maintaining an element of randomness.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

An RPS-focused app could be designed to help users train their unpredictability muscles. Key features might include:

1. AI Opponent: A sophisticated AI that adapts its strategies based on the user's choices, providing a challenging and ever-evolving opponent.
2. Statistics: Tracking the user's wins, losses, and patterns of play to help identify areas for improvement and highlight progress over time.
3. Challenges: A series of increasingly complex challenges that push users to adopt more unpredictable strategies and think critically about their choices.
4. Social Integration: Allowing users to challenge friends or compete in global leaderboards, fostering friendly competition and enhancing the overall gaming experience.
5. Gamification: Implementing rewards and achievements for achieving milestones, such as winning a certain number of games or demonstrating remarkable unpredictability.
6. Tutorials and Tips: Providing educational content about Dennett's ideas and strategies to help users better understand and appreciate the philosophical significance of RPS.
