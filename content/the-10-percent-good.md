# The 10 Percent Good

## Brief Description

The 10 Percent Good is a philosophical tool that encourages focusing on the most valuable aspects of a subject, particularly useful in areas like philosophy where the volume of information can be vast and varied. This approach stems from Sturgeon's Law, a humorous observation that 90% of everything is crud, with only 10% being worthwhile.

## Detailed Description

The 10 Percent Good addresses the challenge of sifting through an overwhelming amount of information to find what truly matters. It seeks to highlight and prioritize the essential elements while discarding the irrelevant or less significant. This approach is particularly relevant in philosophy, where debates can become complex and convoluted, making it challenging to grasp the core ideas.

By focusing on the 10% that matters, individuals can improve their understanding, engagement, and productivity, as they are spending their time and mental energy on what truly contributes to their knowledge and growth. This tool is important because it promotes critical thinking and helps individuals navigate through complex subjects more efficiently.

## Exercise / How to Apply

1. Identify the subject or topic you wish to explore: This could be a philosophical concept, a book, an article, or even a debate.
2. Break down the information into manageable parts: Divide the information into sections or ideas, and try to identify the main points or themes.
3. Evaluate each part: Assess whether each part contributes significantly to your understanding of the subject. If it does not, consider discarding it.
4. Focus on the 10% that matters: Prioritize the most important parts and focus on them, spending more time and mental energy on these crucial elements.
5. Apply this approach consistently: Use this method when engaging with new philosophical ideas or debates to ensure you are focusing on what truly matters.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

Creating an app based on The 10 Percent Good could provide a gamified, interactive experience for users. Here's how it could be structured:

1. Content Library: A collection of philosophical concepts, debates, books, and articles curated by experts and users alike.
2. Sorting Algorithm: An algorithm that evaluates the content based on its relevance and importance, highlighting the 10% that matters most.
3. User Reviews and Ratings: Users can rate and review content, with highly-rated content receiving priority in the library.
4. The Hall of Fame: A section where users can pin their favorite tools, ideas, or debates for easy access and future reference.
5. Discussion Forums: Users can engage in discussions about the content, sharing insights, opinions, and questions with other users.
6. Progress Tracking: An integrated system that tracks user progress, rewarding users for mastering concepts and engaging in discussions.
7. Personalized Recommendations: Based on user preferences, interests, and learning history, the app could offer personalized content recommendations to help users continue learning and growing.

By implementing these features, the app could make The 10 Percent Good approach more accessible, interactive, and engaging for users, helping them navigate complex philosophical concepts with ease and efficiency.
