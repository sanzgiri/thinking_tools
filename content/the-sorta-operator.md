# The Sorta Operator

## Brief Description

The Sorta Operator is a thinking tool proposed by philosopher Daniel Dennett that allows us to discuss gradual evolution and fuzzy boundaries without getting stuck on strict definitions. It acknowledges the presence of entities or concepts that do not fit neatly into predefined categories, as often found in biology and psychology.

## Detailed Description

In his book "Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking," Dennett argues that our obsession with logic has led us to ignore the messy reality of the world. He suggests we adopt a more flexible approach, embracing the idea of fuzzy boundaries and entities that exist in-between categories.

The Sorta Operator is particularly useful when dealing with concepts such as consciousness or life, where clear definitions can be elusive. For instance, a virus is considered "sorta" alive because it replicates but lacks many characteristics typically associated with living organisms. Similarly, a thermostat might not have human-like beliefs, but it does respond to and learn from its environment in a rudimentary way.

By using the Sorta Operator, we can avoid the trap of binary thinking (something is either A or not A) and instead acknowledge the spectrum of possibilities that exist between these extremes. This approach is important because it encourages us to question our assumptions about what counts as a valid entity or concept, allowing for a more nuanced understanding of the world.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice using the Sorta Operator, you can engage in exercises that challenge your preconceived notions about categories and their boundaries. One such exercise could be to classify various entities on a continuous scale, such as consciousness or life. Instead of choosing between True/False options, use a slider to indicate where each entity falls on the spectrum.

For example, you might be asked to place animals like rocks, bacteria, dogs, and humans on a "Consciousness" scale. Your answer would be considered valid as long as it respects the relative order of these entities in terms of their level of consciousness. This exercise helps train your mind to think in shades of grey rather than black and white, fostering a more flexible and open-minded approach to understanding the world.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

An app based on this concept could be designed to make these exercises engaging and interactive. The app might feature various scales related to different categories (e.g., consciousness, life, intelligence) and a range of entities to classify. Users would interact with the app by sliding each entity along the scale to indicate their perceived level of the characteristic being evaluated.

To gamify the concept, the app could offer points for accurate placements, leaderboards to compare scores, and challenges that require users to justify their decisions based on evidence or argumentation. Additionally, the app could include resources for learning more about the entities and categories featured in the exercises, as well as related philosophical concepts.

By creating an enjoyable and educational experience, this app would encourage users to think critically about the boundaries between categories and develop a more nuanced understanding of the world around them, all while having fun doing so.
