# The "Surely" Operator

## Brief Description
The "Surely" Operator is a thinking tool that serves as a warning sign for weak arguments, particularly when used by philosophers or intellectuals to assert their points with an air of certainty. It often signals a moment where the author leans on intuition and social pressure instead of providing a robust argument.

## Detailed Description
Daniel Dennett, in his book "Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking," warns against relying on the word "surely" (or synonyms like "obviously," "clearly") as it can be a mental block designed to prevent readers from questioning or requesting evidence. This operator is used to make statements seem self-evident, appealing to the reader's consensus rather than presenting a well-reasoned argument.

For instance, consider a philosophical debate where one participant states, "Surely we all agree that morality is universal." While the statement may feel intuitive, it lacks concrete evidence or reasoning, making it a potential weak point in the argument. The "Surely" Operator serves to highlight such moments, encouraging critical thinking and pushing for more rigorous arguments.

## Exercise / How to Apply
To practice identifying the "Surely" Operator, follow these steps:
1. Engage with philosophical texts or debates, either written or oral.
2. Pay close attention when you encounter phrases like "surely," "obviously," or "clearly."
3. Evaluate whether there is sufficient evidence provided to support the claim. If not, consider why the author might have used such a phrase and whether it serves as a mental block against further questioning.
4. Reframe the argument by asking for evidence or reasoning that supports the claim, even if you personally believe it to be self-evident. This practice will help cultivate a habit of rigorous thinking.

## Suggestion for Creating an App
To gamify the concept of the "Surely" Operator, consider developing an app with the following features:
1. A speed-reading exercise that rapidly scrolls philosophical texts. Users must tap a button every time they encounter the word "surely" (or synonyms). Upon tapping, the text pauses, and the user is prompted to predict why the author used it ("Is there evidence here? Y/N").
2. Progress tracking and leaderboards to foster competition among users, encouraging them to improve their critical thinking skills over time.
3. A discussion forum where users can debate and analyze the passages they encounter, fostering a community of critical thinkers who practice identifying and challenging weak arguments.
4. Regular updates with new philosophical texts to keep the content fresh and engaging.

By combining fun gameplay mechanics with essential philosophical concepts, this app could help cultivate critical thinking skills in an enjoyable way.
