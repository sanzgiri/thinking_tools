# Three Species of Goulding

## Brief Description

Daniel Dennett, in his book "Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking", categorizes rhetorical fallacies used by Stephen Jay Gould, his rival in evolutionary theory, into three types: Rathering, Piling On, and The Gould Two-Step. These techniques are employed to mislead, confuse, or overpower an audience through deceptive argumentation.

## Detailed Description

### Rathering
Rathering presents a false dichotomy where the author critiques position A and offers position B as the alternative ("It is rather B"), when in fact B is compatible with A. This fallacy is used to create an illusion of choice, but in reality, it constrains the discussion within predetermined boundaries. For example, a creationist might argue that evolutionary theory can't explain the complexity of the human eye and therefore, intelligent design is a better explanation ("It is rather Intelligent Design"). However, this argument oversimplifies the vast body of evidence supporting the gradual development of complex structures through natural selection.

### Piling On
Piling On involves listing disparate objections to a theory to create an impression of overwhelming failure, even if the objections are contradictory or trivial. This technique is used to discredit a theory by flooding the debate with unrelated criticisms and making it seem indefensible. An example would be attacking the Big Bang Theory based on its inability to explain dark matter (a valid criticism), but then proceeding to criticize it for not explaining the origin of gravity (an invalid criticism, as gravity is a well-understood force in physics).

### The Gould Two-Step
The Gould Two-Step involves attacking a "Straw Man" version of a theory to discredit it, then retreating to a "Motte and Bailey" defense when challenged. This fallacy allows the author to claim the moral high ground while simultaneously undermining their opponents' arguments. For instance, a proponent of creationism might argue that evolutionary theory supports atheism (a misrepresentation of the theory), but when confronted with evidence against this claim, they assert that their belief in God is a matter of faith, not science (the "Motte", or main position, which is inaccessible to criticism).

## Exercise / How to Apply
To practice identifying and combating the Three Species of Goulding, users can engage in critical thinking exercises. For example, read scientific debates or political speeches and highlight instances where these fallacies are employed. Tag each instance with the specific "Goulding" species for a more accurate analysis. This exercise not only improves one's ability to recognize deceptive argumentation but also encourages the development of logical reasoning skills.
