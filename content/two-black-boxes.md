# Two Black Boxes

## Brief Description

Two Black Boxes is a thinking tool that emphasizes function over mechanism, stating that if two entities behave identically, they can be considered equivalent from an intentional stance. This tool serves to solve the problem of attributing mind or consciousness to complex systems by focusing on their observable behaviors rather than their internal workings.

## Detailed Description

Daniel Dennett introduced Two Black Boxes as a part of his philosophical approach known as functionalism. Functionalism posits that an entity's mental states can be understood in terms of their function or behavior, independent of the specific neural structures or processes responsible for these functions. In other words, if two entities produce identical outputs given the same inputs, they are considered to have the same internal states.

Two Black Boxes is particularly relevant when dealing with complex systems, such as artificial intelligence (AI), where understanding the intricate workings of the system may be difficult or impossible. By focusing on the functional similarities between two entities, we can avoid making unwarranted assumptions about their inner workings and instead judge them based on their observable behavior.

An example of Two Black Boxes is the Turing Test, where a human interacts with two unseen entities - one human and one AI - and attempts to determine which is which. If the human cannot distinguish between the two, it implies that the AI has passed the test, demonstrating functional equivalence between the two entities.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice the Two Black Boxes thinking tool, follow these steps:

1. Observe two systems (either physical or abstract) that perform similar functions despite having different internal structures or mechanisms.
2. Analyze their behavior under various conditions and attempt to identify any discrepancies in their output.
3. If the two systems behave identically, consider them functionally equivalent, regardless of their internal differences.
4. Apply this approach when encountering complex systems, such as AI agents or even other human beings, to avoid making unwarranted assumptions about their inner workings and instead focus on their observable behavior.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

An app based on Two Black Boxes could be designed to help users practice this thinking tool in a fun and interactive way. Here are some potential features:

1. Chatbot Interaction: Users can engage in conversations with AI agents and human participants, aiming to identify which is which through their responses. The goal is to reinforce the idea of functional equivalence between humans and advanced AI.
2. Quiz Mode: Users could answer a series of questions about different systems (e.g., an AI, a beehive, or a flock of birds) based on their observed behavior. The app would provide hints about the internal workings of each system to encourage users to focus on function over mechanism.
3. Gamification: Points, badges, and leaderboards could incentivize users to engage with the app regularly, reinforcing the Two Black Boxes concept in a playful manner.
4. Real-world Examples: The app could provide examples of functional equivalence in everyday life, encouraging users to apply the Two Black Boxes thinking tool beyond the digital realm.
