# Two Lotteries

## Brief Description
The Two Lotteries thinking tool is a philosophical exercise designed to illustrate that determinism (the belief that past events determine all future events) does not render our choices meaningless. It compares the experience and outcome of winning a lottery drawn before buying a ticket versus after, both with equal odds.

## Detailed Description
Dennett introduces this tool in his book "Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking" as a way to challenge our intuitive assumptions about free will and determinism. The exercise demonstrates that winning the lottery, even if predestined, does not negate the joy or significance of the win. This concept is known as the Open Future – the possibility for new, unpredictable events to occur despite the existence of deterministic laws.

In our everyday lives, we often feel as though our choices matter and that we have some level of control over our outcomes. However, when faced with seemingly predetermined situations, like a lottery win, we may question this sense of agency. The Two Lotteries exercise helps us reconsider our assumptions about free will by showing that even in predestined scenarios, the outcome can still feel meaningful and satisfying.

## Exercise / How to Apply
To practice the Two Lotteries thinking tool:
1. Imagine or simulate a lottery with equal odds of winning.
2. Visualize or experience the sensation of winning this lottery both after buying a ticket (post-draw) and before buying a ticket (pre-draw).
3. Reflect on the feelings, emotions, and significance attached to each scenario.
4. Recognize that in both cases, you still feel joy, excitement, or a sense of accomplishment.
5. Apply this insight to your everyday life, understanding that even if circumstances appear predetermined, your choices and actions can still carry meaning and have consequences.

## Suggestion for Creating an App
An app based on the Two Lotteries concept could be designed as a gamified experience to help users practice this thinking tool more effectively:

1. **Lottery Simulator**: Users could choose from various lottery types with adjustable odds to simulate different scenarios.
2. **Two Lotteries Mode**: This mode would allow users to experience winning both the pre-draw and post-draw lotteries, comparing the emotional responses in each case.
3. **Reflective Questions**: After each simulation, users could be presented with thought-provoking questions designed to encourage reflection on their feelings and the implications for free will.
4. **Progress Tracking**: Users could track their progress over time, observing patterns in their reactions and gaining a deeper understanding of their intuitive assumptions about free will and determinism.
5. **Discussion Forums**: Including discussion forums or peer-to-peer interaction would facilitate the sharing of insights and ideas, helping users refine their understanding of these complex philosophical concepts.
