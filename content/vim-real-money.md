# Vim: Real Money?

## Brief Description

"Vim: Real Money?" is a thought tool inspired by philosopher Daniel Dennett's idea of Qualia, which can be understood as the subjective, direct experiences that make up our individual consciousness. This tool encourages us to recognize and avoid category errors in thinking, particularly when it comes to understanding the nature of our mental states and their relationship with physical reality.

## Detailed Description

In Dennett's perspective, our minds create an internal currency, similar to Vim in a game, to understand and navigate the world. This internal currency, or Qualia, includes sensations like pain, colors, sounds, emotions, and thoughts. While these experiences are deeply real to us, they don't have direct equivalents in the physical world. Asking "How much is Vim worth in real money?" is a category error because Vim is a mental construct with no tangible value outside of the game. Similarly, questioning "What is pain worth in physics?" misses the mark since pain is a subjective experience, not a measurable physical quantity.

The importance of this tool lies in promoting clear thinking and helping us understand the limits and nature of our consciousness. By recognizing that our internal experiences are distinct from physical reality, we can better appreciate the complexity and richness of our mental lives while avoiding confusion and misconceptions.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice this tool, imagine you've earned Points in a game or accumulated a collection of virtual items. Your desire is to exchange these Points for real-world goods or services. However, no matter how hard you try, the system refuses to convert your Points into tangible assets. Instead, it reminds you that Points only have value within the game. This exercise encourages you to realize that while your mental states (like Points) are deeply real to you, they don't always translate directly into the physical world.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

To build a mobile or web app based on this tool, consider creating an interactive game where users earn Points by completing various tasks or challenges. The app could allow users to try cashing out their Points for real-world items, only to be informed that Points have no physical value and can only be used within the game.

The app could also incorporate educational elements, such as explanations of the philosophical concepts behind Qualia and category errors. Additionally, it could include exercises or puzzles designed to help users practice recognizing and avoiding these errors in their everyday thinking. To make the experience more engaging, consider gamifying these exercises by awarding bonus Points or other rewards for correct answers or successful completions.
