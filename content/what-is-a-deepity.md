# What Is a Deepity?

## Brief Description
A deepity is a statement that appears profound but actually equivocates between two meanings, one being true but trivial, and the other profound but false or nonsensical.

## Detailed Description
In his book "Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking", philosopher Daniel Dennett introduces the concept of a deepity to help us navigate our thinking around seemingly profound statements. A deepity arises when we unintentionally switch between two different meanings of a statement, leading us to perceive it as more profound than it actually is.

The first meaning (Meaning A) is typically a straightforward, true, yet unremarkable interpretation. For example, in the statement "Love is just a word", this could be understood as love being an expression that we use in our language. The second meaning (Meaning B) is usually a deeper, more philosophical understanding of the concept, which may be false or nonsensical. In the same example, Meaning B might imply that love holds no real significance beyond its verbal representation, which is patently false.

The problem deepities solve is our tendency to fall for grand-sounding statements without thoroughly evaluating their implications. By identifying deepities, we can sharpen our critical thinking skills and avoid being swayed by superficial profundity.

## Exercise / How to Apply
To practice identifying deepities, follow these steps:
1. Pay attention to statements that seem profound but leave you feeling confused or unsure about their meaning.
2. Break down the statement into two interpretations. One should be a straightforward, true, and unremarkable understanding (Trivial/True), while the other could be a more profound interpretation that might be false or nonsensical (Profound/False).
3. Evaluate each interpretation carefully to ensure you understand what they are saying. If both interpretations are true but one is trivial and the other profound, then you have a deepity on your hands.
4. Practice this exercise regularly with different statements, especially those found in popular media like social media posts, self-help books, or inspiring quotes.
