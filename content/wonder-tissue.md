# Wonder Tissue

## Brief Description

The Wonder Tissue thinking tool is a philosophical jest created by Daniel Dennett to satirize dualistic perspectives that propose consciousness is composed of a unique substance with magical properties, not found in ordinary matter. It encourages materialism, the belief that only physical substances exist and can explain all phenomena, including mental states.

## Detailed Description

Dualists often argue that consciousness or soul cannot be fully explained by neurons, chemicals, and electricity, as they possess qualities like immateriality, self-awareness, and non-physical interaction. To mock these ideas, Dennett humorously proposes "Wonder Tissue" — a hypothetical magical substance responsible for consciousness that dualists imply must exist. Materialists, however, believe that mental states are the result of complex interactions within the brain, and no special substance is required to explain consciousness.

This philosophical tool highlights the importance of maintaining a materialist approach towards understanding the mind. By emphasizing that even seemingly magical or immaterial concepts can be explained by natural processes, we can avoid unnecessary metaphysical speculation and instead focus on empirical evidence and scientific research.

## Exercise / How to Apply

To practice the Wonder Tissue thinking tool, begin by questioning dualistic claims about consciousness. When you encounter ideas suggesting that the mind is made of a special substance or magic, challenge those assumptions with materialist alternatives.

One practical exercise is the "Materialist Scanner" game:

1. Examine a brain diagram and identify areas responsible for specific mental functions, such as memory or emotion.
2. Avoid clicking on generic glowing orbs labeled as "Soul" or "Memory." These represent the dualistic perspective of consciousness being a separate entity from physical matter.
3. Instead, locate the hippocampus or other brain regions known to be involved in memory processes. The synaptic web connecting these neurons represents the materialist understanding of memory.

By consistently applying this exercise, you train your mind to seek materialist explanations for complex phenomena and resist dualistic notions that rely on magical substances like Wonder Tissue.

## Suggestion for Creating an App

An app based on the "Wonder Tissue" thinking tool could be designed to teach users about materialism, while also challenging their dualistic preconceptions in a fun and interactive way. Here are some features it might include:

1. **Brain Diagram Interactive**: A detailed brain diagram where users can click on various regions to learn more about their functions. This helps reinforce the idea that the brain is responsible for mental states, not a magical substance like Wonder Tissue.
2. **Memory Match Game**: A "Where's Waldo" style game where users must find the source of memory within a complex brain diagram. This reinforces the materialist perspective and encourages critical thinking when identifying dualistic misconceptions.
3. **Dualism Debunking Challenges**: Short, engaging scenarios presenting common dualistic arguments about consciousness. Users must then provide materialist explanations for these phenomena to earn points and progress in the game.
4. **Materialist Quotes & Videos**: Inspirational quotes and educational videos from philosophers, neuroscientists, and other experts on materialism to motivate users and deepen their understanding of the concept.
5. **Leaderboards and Achievements**: Gamify the learning process by offering leaderboards, achievements, and badges for completing levels, debunking arguments, and mastering materialist concepts.

By creating an app that gamifies the Wonder Tissue thinking tool, users can learn about materialism in a fun and engaging way while simultaneously challenging their dualistic biases.
